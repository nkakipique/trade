Ministry of Trade & Industry

The Ministry of Investment, Trade, and Industry is focused on creating a positive environment for investment, sustainable industries, and trade to foster economic diversification and growth. Its key objectives include promoting sustainable industries, diversifying the economy through export development, attracting both foreign and domestic investments, generating wealth, fostering employment, empowering citizens economically, promoting entrepreneurship, and eradicating poverty.

Welcome To Ministry Of Trade & Industry.

Honorable Minister Mmusi Kgafela

Mmusi Kgafela is the Member of Parliament for Mochudi West Constituency and currently serves as the Honourable Minister of Trade and Industry in the Corporate Services department. With a legal background holding a Bachelor of Laws, he entered active national politics in 2017 through the Botswana Democratic Party. Kgafela has a notable legal career as an Attorney of the High Court, specializing in conveyancing and notary public services. His political journey includes serving as the Minister of Infrastructure and Housing Development from October 2019 to May 2021.

Ass.Minister Beauty Manake

Hon. Beauty M. Manake, Botswana's Assistant Minister of Trade & Industry, brings over 17 years of experience in trade and investment. With degrees in Information Systems, Food and Agribusiness Management, and an MBA in Agricultural Business and Marketing, she started as a Systems Technologist before transitioning to agribusiness.
A prominent figure in global forums, she successfully campaigned for Botswana to host the 2023 IAGR, setting a world attendance record. Her diverse expertise in business development and CSR has contributed significantly to Botswana's international investment reputation.

PS Ellen Richard-Madisa

Ellen Richard-Madisa, the current Permanent Secretary in the Ministry of Trade and Industry, brings over three decades of experience. Starting at the Bank of Botswana in 1991, she progressed through key roles in finance and administration within the Ministry of Finance and Development Planning.
Her expertise includes financial sector regulation, procurement policy, and anti-money laundering activities. With a Master's in Commerce in Economics, she has been actively involved in various administrative boards for organizations such as BITC, SEZA, Mineral Development Company Botswana, and Debswana Diamond Company.
